Her Story

Meet Posie

The small rescue cat who inspired everything we grow.

How It Began

She came from under the cottage.

We first spotted her as a shadow — a small, cautious shape tucked beneath the back steps of the cottage. She was thin, wary, and clearly on her own. We set out a feeding station and waited.

Day by day she came closer. She watched us tend the raised beds, patrol the zinnias, deadhead the dahlias. She seemed to be deciding whether we were worth trusting. Eventually, she decided we were — on her own terms, in her own time.

We named her Posie, for the little bouquets of wildflowers she reminded us of: small, unexpected, and impossible to overlook.

The Ice Storm

She endured the storm.

That winter brought one of the worst ice storms the county had seen in years. We worried about her — whether she had found somewhere dry, somewhere safe. She reappeared on the third day, frost on her whiskers, hunger in her eyes, but otherwise unharmed.

It was the storm that changed everything between us. After that, she let us closer. She accepted warmth and shelter, began to sit at the garden’s edge while we worked, following us row to row with a quiet, watchful presence.

“She seemed drawn to the flowers, curling up to nap among the zinnias and dahlias, as if the blooms were her sanctuary.”
Her Sanctuary

The blooms were her sanctuary.

As the seasons turned, Posie became an inseparable part of the garden’s rhythm. She had her spots — a patch of soft soil between the dahlia rows, a warm stone near the lavender, the corner of the cottage steps in the afternoon sun.

But as another winter neared, we noticed her slowing. Vet visits confirmed what we had quietly feared: her health was declining. We gave her every comfort the garden and our hearts could offer — warmth, stillness, the smell of growing things.

When it was time to say goodbye, we held her in the garden she loved. The dahlias were still in bloom.

Her Legacy

Her legacy blooms on.

After Posie passed, Gracen needed something to do with the grief and the love and the strange quiet the garden held without her. She started building — products, art, a brand — a way to keep Posie’s presence alive in the garden and in the hands of people who understand what it means to love a small creature deeply.

The Posie line was born from that impulse. Bouquets named after her. Art prints featuring the flowers she slept among. Gifts meant to carry meaning forward, the way she carried herself through the garden — quietly, fully, with her whole self.

“Posie’s line of products is my way of honoring her — blooms grown in the gardens in which she spent her time — a reminder that even the most fleeting lives can leave a lasting imprint.”

— Gracen
