Flower Care Questions

How do I keep my flowers fresh as long as possible?

The moment you get home, trim about a half-inch from the bottom of each stem at a 45-degree angle — this gives the stems more surface area to drink. Place them immediately in clean water in a cool spot away from direct sun and ripening fruit. Change the water every other day and re-trim the stems each time. Your blooms should last 5–10 days depending on variety.

Why are my flowers wilting even though I've been watering them?

Wilting is often less about water and more about the stem's ability to uptake it. Bacteria in the vase water can block the stem ends, preventing absorption even when submerged. Try a full refresh: rinse the vase thoroughly, add clean cold water, re-cut the stems, and remove any leaves that sit below the waterline — those rot quickly and foul the water. If wilting is severe, try submerging the entire flower head gently in cool water for 20–30 minutes to rehydrate it.
